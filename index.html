Prof. Kojo Fosu

Pioneer of African Art Studies | Historian | Educator | Cultural Visionary

Biography

Prof. Kojo Fosu was a distinguished Ghanaian art historian, educator, and cultural advocate. He laid the foundation for the academic study of African art with decades of research, teaching, and creative leadership. His vision helped reshape how the world understands traditional and modern African aesthetics.

Published Works

  • Books: 4 influential books including 20th Century Art of Africa (1986, revised 1993) and Traditional Art of Ghana (1994)
  • Journal Articles: 12 peer-reviewed publications on African cultural expression
  • Catalogues: 30 art exhibition catalogues documenting African visual heritage

Seminars & Lectures

  • 12 scholarly papers presented at international seminars across the USA, Europe, and Africa
  • 20 lectures delivered at major academic and cultural institutions globally
